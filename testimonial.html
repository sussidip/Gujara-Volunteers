HUMANITARIAN / VOLUNTEERING WORK

Awareness about early child marriage

In 2025, Gujara Volunteers, a grassroots organization dedicated to social justice, is launching a transformative campaign to combat early child marriage in vulnerable communities. Recognizing the devastating consequences of this practice—including disrupted education, health risks, and perpetuated cycles of poverty and gender inequality—the campaign seeks to ignite systemic change through awareness, education, and community empowerment.

Free Tuition 2025

In 2025, the Gujara Volunteers continue their unwavering commitment to empowering underprivileged students through their groundbreaking Free Tuition Initiative. This community-driven program is designed to bridge educational gaps and provide equitable learning opportunities for children and youth across rural and urban areas, regardless of socioeconomic background.

Health awareness program

In 2024, the Gujara Volunteers are launching a transformative Health Awareness Program aimed at fostering a healthier, more informed community across Gujarat. This initiative underscores their commitment to bridging gaps in healthcare access and education, with a focus on preventive care, mental health, nutrition, and disease management. Designed for all age groups, the program prioritizes inclusivity, ensuring underserved populations—women, children, seniors, and rural communities—receive tailored support.

Mat distribution to sit in winter

In 2024, Gujara Volunteers organized a Winter Mat Distribution Program to support underprivileged families and students during the cold season. Recognizing the challenges faced by many in accessing proper seating and insulation in winter, the volunteers distributed warm mats to provide comfort and protection from the cold. This initiative particularly benefited students, elderly individuals, and low-income families, ensuring they had a warm and comfortable place to sit during daily activities.

Free Tuition 2024

The Free Tuition Program 2024, organized by Gujara Volunteers, aimed to provide quality education and academic support to underprivileged students in the community. Understanding the financial barriers that many families face, this initiative offered free tutoring sessions to help students improve their learning outcomes and excel in their studies. Volunteer educators provided assistance in various subjects, ocusing on fundamental concepts, exam preparation, and interactive learning methods.

Donate ball and jersey to a club

In 2024, Gujara Volunteers organized a Sports Equipment Donation Program to support local athletes and promote sports development in the community. As part of this initiative, the volunteers donated footballs and jerseys to a local club, encouraging young players to engage in sports and enhance their skills. The program aimed to provide essential resources for the club, ensuring that financial limitations do not hinder talented players from pursuing their passion for football.

Stationary distribution among needy students

The Stationery Distribution Program 2025, organized by Gujara Volunteers, aimed to support the education of underprivileged students by providing them with essential learning materials. This initiative focused on ensuring that every child, regardless of their financial background, has access to the necessary tools for their academic growth.

Plant distribution among village students

The Plant Distribution Program 2025, organized by Gujara Volunteers, aimed to promote environmental awareness and sustainability among village students. As part of this initiative, young learners were provided with saplings and educated on the importance of afforestation, biodiversity conservation, and eco-friendly practices.

Parents Awareness Program

The Parents Awareness Program 2024, organized by Gujara Volunteers, aimed to educate parents about key social, educational, and health-related issues affecting children and families. The program focused on fostering a supportive home environment, enhancing parental involvement in education, and promoting awareness of modern challenges such as digital safety, mental health, and substance abuse prevention.

Two Dollar Toilet

In 2009, the dedicated volunteers of Gujara embarked on a remarkable mission to address a critical sanitation issue by introducing the revolutionary Two Dollar Toilet initiative. Recognizing the dire need for improved sanitation facilities in marginalized communities, these volunteers worked tirelessly to design and implement a cost-effective solution. The Two Dollar Toilet project aimed to provide affordable and accessible toilet facilities, contributing not only to public health but also to the dignity and well-being of individuals.
